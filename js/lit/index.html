рҹҢё︎ higasi [WIP]

higasi (аІ¬аІҝаІ—аІёаІҝ) is the primary language of the peninsular country of higasi oka (аІ¬аІҝаІ—аІёаІҝ аІ’аІ•), whose territory extends to a number of islands to the northeast of the ГёstendЕ«nska imperium. the language, culture, and much of the geography of higasi oka is based on our world's japan; however, the writing system is instead that of kannada, from the indian state of karnataka.

while this is an instance of using a real-world language to save effort on constructing languages, some alterations are still made to give higasi its own identity apart from japanese. first, the pronunciation is based on the underlying phonemes, rather than dialectical phonetics; e.g. вҖ№гҒЎгӮғвҖә is pronounced as /tja/ rather than the [tЙ•a] realized via yod-coalescence in the "standard" tokyo dialect. secondly, kanji are, whenever possible, read as their kun'yomi; e.g. the compound пҪўзө¶еҜҫпҪЈ would normally be read as пҪўгҒңгҒЈгҒҹгҒ„пҪЈ (zettai) based on its on'yomi, but here it's read as пҪўгҒҹгҒӮгҒ„гҒҰпҪЈ (taaite). finally, as the stories in this world are set in eras approximately equivalent to the 17th to early 20th centuries, historic vocabulary is preferable... though that could just be my late onset дёӯдәҢз—… (here read as пҪўгҒӘгҒӢгҒөгҒҹгӮ„пҪЈ, where the пҪўгҒөпҪЈ is obviously pronounced as /hu/ rather than [ЙёЙҜ]).
